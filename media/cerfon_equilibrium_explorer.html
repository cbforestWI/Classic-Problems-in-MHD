Cerfon Equilibrium Explorer

This browser-side solver uses the up-down symmetric Cerfon/Freidberg Solov’ev basis from the Grad–Shafranov lecture. Adjust the shaping parameters, source-mix parameter, and engineering scales to compare the target boundary against the fitted U = 0 last closed flux surface, the interior flux contours, and a few lecture-style equilibrium figures of merit.

Inverse aspect ratio is ε = a/R0, so smaller values here produce wider plasmas.

The plotted equilibrium is U(X,Y) = Up(X,Y) + Σj=06 cj Uj(X,Y), with the coefficients fixed by the outer point, inner point, top point, and local-curvature constraints described in the lecture.
This is a teaching-focused explorer, not a production reconstruction code. It keeps the symmetric polynomial basis from the lecture and uses a simple contouring pass in the browser.

If the fit becomes visibly poor, try easing the shape back toward moderate triangularity of either sign or larger R0/a. Extreme shapes can strain this seven-coefficient symmetric basis.
